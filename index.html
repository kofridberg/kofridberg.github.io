I am a 4th year undergraduate pursuing a degree in chemistry & physics and math at Harvard University. Originally from Boulder, CO, I like to spend my time working on research projects based on questions and observations that pique my curiosity. I also enjoy rock climbing and running.

Research Interests

I am passionate about solving (and developing mathematical methods for solving) analysis problems that originate in biology, chemistry, and physics. I enjoy using both computational and theoretical methods.

Some of my previous research projects (see Resume for details) have been:

  • studying a beautiful polygonal spiral produced by a sequence of increasing n-gons (euclidean geometry / analysis)
  • developing cellular automata simulations to explore population dynamics in mutualistic microbial communities (computational biology)
  • hardware-software interfacing to optimize microwave pulse generation for SiV spin qubits (quantum information science)
  • synthesizing and crystallizing a novel mixed metal sulfate compound using a natural mineral precursor (inorganic chemistry)
